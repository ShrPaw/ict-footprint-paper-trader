## KEY FINDINGS

### Surviving Candidates (Ranked)

1. **SOL / Higher-Low Uptrend** — 1,712 events, mean=+0.59%, t=5.02, +rate=51.9%, subsample 100%. Works across ALL regimes. Strongest overall candidate.

2. **BTC / Displacement Bullish** — 455 events, mean=+0.57%, t=4.14, +rate=55.4%, subsample 100%. Positive in ALL 5 years (most directionally consistent).

3. **ETH / Displacement Bullish** — 431 events, mean=+0.58%, t=3.15, +rate=53.1%, subsample 100%. Regime-specific edge in VOL_EXP (+1.54%).

4. **SOL / Stop-Run in Trending Up** — 170 events, mean=+1.29%, t=2.74, +rate=51.8%, subsample 100%. Highest mean but smallest sample.

5. **XRP / Post-Bear Volume Surge** — 1,415 events, mean=+0.45%, t=3.40, +rate=52.7%, subsample 100%. Best quarterly consistency (82%).

### What Failed (Everything Else)
- Breakout events: 55-60% clustering, time-split unstable
- ATR spike events: 88-92% clustering (volatility clusters)
- Volume squeeze: 89-91% clustering
- EMA cross + momentum: not significant
- Killzone momentum: not significant
- All volatility transitions: clustering or instability

### Key Insight
**Structural price patterns work. Raw volatility/volume metrics don't.** The market at 1h is not predictable from breakouts, volatility transitions, or volume-only signals. Only price structure (higher lows, displacement candles, stop runs) captures real supply/demand shifts.

### Critical Caveats
- No candidate passes strict year-by-year significance (40% of years individually significant — power issue from splitting 4yr data)
- 2022 consistently weak across all candidates (crypto winter regime)
- These are raw forward returns, not risk-adjusted trading returns
- Highest-mean candidates have smallest samples
